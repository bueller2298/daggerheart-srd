# FANEWICK DANGER BANDS

This note defines three escalating danger “bands” for the Fanewick region using the **SRD Witherwild map** (the one with the name-suggestion callout box) and the vault’s **Mapfile (colored version)** as the practical table reference.

## Core Rule: Distance + Edge = Danger
**Farther from population centers and nearer the map edges = deadlier encounters and stronger Witherwild warping.**

Use this as the baseline even when local conditions are calm: the deeper and more remote the party goes, the more likely it is that reality is unreliable, navigation becomes costly, and threats escalate in lethality and strangeness.

## Political Overlay: Haven Routes vs Deep Wild
- **Closer to Haven routes/holdings:** threats trend **militarized, organized, human-made** (patrols, interdictions, conscription gangs, “lawful” violence, supply-line conflict).
- **Deeper wild / away from routes:** threats trend **divinity-touched, Withered, and reality-warped** (omens, curses, living landscapes, predatory spirits, ecological horror).

---

# INNER BAND
*“The breathing edge of civilization.”*

**How to identify it on the map**
- Near **named roads/travel corridors** and **settled points** on the Mapfile.
- Locations where a traveler can plausibly find shelter, trade, or a patrol within a day.

## Typical factions present
- **Haven-aligned forces:** road patrols, levy troops, scouts, checkpoint keepers, quartermasters.
- **Local authorities & operators:** town guards, wardens, toll-collectors, bailiffs, mercenary “security.”
- **Civilians & commerce:** caravans, ferrymen, traders, messengers, pilgrims, refugees.
- **Low-level opportunists:** bandits who prefer intimidation and ransom over slaughter.

## Corruption intensity
- **Low → Moderate.**
- The Witherwild “shows its teeth,” but still obeys enough rules that people can live nearby.

## Encounter tone
- **Tense and political.** Violence is often *avoidable* or *negotiable*—but consequences are social.
- Conflicts are frequently about **jurisdiction, supplies, accusations, protection rackets, fugitives**, or “who owns the road.”

## Common Tier ranges
- **Tier 1 is common. Tier 2 appears as spikes or leadership.**

---

# MID BAND
*“Where the wild becomes a force with opinions.”*

**How to identify it on the map**
- Between the safer corridors and the farthest reaches.
- Settlements (if any) are isolated, seasonal, or half-abandoned.
- Travel is possible, but it’s **costly** (time, supplies, Stress, detours, bad bargains).

## Typical factions present
- **Frontier actors:** trappers, hermits, scavengers, monster-hunters, renegade scouts.
- **Displaced & desperate:** refugees in hiding, deserters, outlaw enclaves.
- **Occult/ritual groups:** bargain-makers, “helpers” who demand prices, secret-keepers.
- **Haven’s long arm (thin):** small patrols, special investigators, bounty agents.

## Corruption intensity
- **Moderate → High.**
- Paths shift, landmarks move, memories fray; the land punishes certainty.

## Encounter tone
- **Survival-horror and moral pressure.**
- The environment participates: terrain, weather, and “wrongness” are meaningful obstacles.

## Common Tier ranges
- **Tier 2 is common. Tier 3 appears as set-piece threats or region rulers.**

---

# EDGE BAND
*“The Witherwild’s deep country—where rules are negotiable and the price is you.”*

**How to identify it on the map**
- The farthest, most remote map regions and especially anything **near the outer map edges**.
- Areas where returning “the same way you came” is uncertain without preparation or a guide.

## Typical factions present
- **Withered and divinity-touched:** spirits, saints-as-hazards, living myths, hungry miracles.
- **Apex predators & ancient things:** entities too old, too large, or too symbolic to be “just monsters.”
- **Fanewick anomalies:** wardens and balancers that feel like curses.
- **Rare Haven intrusion:** elite teams, catastrophic retreats, ruthless extraction operations.

## Corruption intensity
- **High → Extreme.**
- Reality warps are not events—they’re *weather*.

## Encounter tone
- **Deadly, surreal, and consequential.**
- “Win” often means **survive, escape, seal, appease, or redirect**, not “defeat.”

## Common Tier ranges
- **Tier 3 is common. Tier 4 appears as capstones, guardians, or campaign-pivot entities.**

# Mapfile Anchors
Use these as quick “known points” to place the party, then classify everything else by **proximity to Haven routes/settlements** and **distance toward the map edge**.

## Inner Anchors (known)
- **[[Haven]]** (population center / political gravity well)
- **[[Wood’s End]]** *(if used as a settlement/edge-of-civilization staging point)*
- Any **named road junctions, ferries, and patrol outposts** you’ve placed along Haven routes

## Mid Anchors (known)
- **[[Fanewick]]** (as a region-wide label; most “true Fanewick travel” begins in Mid)
- **[[Tree Spire]]** (Southwest Fanewick; “refuge-by-day / threat-by-night” site)
- **[[Alula]] / [[Alulan Archival Atelier]]** *(if placed as an isolated research enclave or ruin-site)*

## Edge Anchors (known)
- **[[Isle of the Twelve]]** (wild, divinity-saturated; use as Edge unless explicitly “civilized”)
- Any **outer coastline**, **far borderlands**, and **edge-adjacent landmarks** on the Mapfile

### Fast classification rule
- If it’s **within a day of patrols and trade** → **Inner**
- If it’s **reachable but costly, and the land interferes** → **Mid**
- If it’s **remote enough that maps lie** → **Edge**

# Escalation Knobs by Band
Flip these mid-session to keep the band “feeling right” without rewriting encounters.

## Inner escalation knobs
1) **Reinforcements arrive** (organized response, arrests, backup, alarms)
2) **Supply friction** (tolls, rationing, curfews, seized gear, permits)
3) **Jurisdiction conflict** (two authorities claim control; party becomes leverage)
4) **Low weirdness spikes** (thin-spot event: brief warp, omen, sudden blight)
5) **Collateral consequences** (violence triggers warrants, feuds, political fallout)

## Mid escalation knobs
1) **Navigation tax** (the “correct” path costs Stress/Time/Resources; the “easy” path costs Hope/Morality)
2) **Terrain hostility** (difficult ground, choking brambles, predatory bogs, collapsing routes)
3) **Bargain pressure** (helpful entity offers a shortcut for a price)
4) **Stalker logic** (something follows; it learns; it waits for weakness)
5) **Isolation** (no safe rest; safety requires tradeoffs; shelter comes with strings)

## Edge escalation knobs
1) **Reality weather** (time slip, distance distortion, names-as-prices, memory bleed)
2) **Mythic attention** (the region “notices” the PCs; omens escalate; encounters become personal)
3) **Hard fail states** (escape routes close; bargains are binding; mistakes echo forward)
4) **Environmental bosses** (the place itself has “turns”: lair actions, moving hazards, living terrain)
5) **Divinity/Withered surge** (miracle-as-threat; “blessing” that costs; Withered manifestation)

# Tier Budget Heuristic (Party of 3 PCs default)
Use this to keep band difficulty consistent at the table. Adjust up/down if your party is larger/smaller.

## Inner (Tier 1–2)
- **Default fights:** mostly **Tier 1**
- **Leader spike:** one **Tier 2** with Tier 1 support
- **If it’s “deadly Inner”:** it should feel *organized*, not *mythic* (numbers, tactics, reinforcements)

## Mid (Tier 2–3)
- **Default fights:** mostly **Tier 2**
- **Set-piece threats:** one **Tier 3** (solo or with light Tier 2 support)
- **If it’s “deadly Mid”:** the land participates (hazards, pursuit, resource drain) even if enemies aren’t Tier 3+

## Edge (Tier 3–4)
- **Default fights:** mostly **Tier 3**
- **Capstones:** **Tier 4** as rare apex/campaign-pivot entities
- **If it’s “deadly Edge”:** victory conditions shift away from “defeat everything” toward **survive/escape/seal/appease**

### Band-consistent “feel” check
Before you run a scene, answer:
1) **Is the threat organized (Haven-route) or metaphysical (deep wild)?**
2) **How much does the environment participate (none / meaningful / dominant)?**
3) **Is violence avoidable, and what does avoidance cost?**
4) **Does the Tier match the band’s expectation, or is this a deliberate spike?**

If those answers fit the band, the encounter will feel right even if the exact stat blocks vary.
