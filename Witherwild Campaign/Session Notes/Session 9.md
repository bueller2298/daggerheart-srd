# SESSION 9 — THE FIELDS OF UNCEASING SONG

**Act:** Act 2 (opening)  
**Band:** Mid → Edge (transition session)  
**Session goal (GM):** Teach that the land has “rules,” then give them a costly but real route toward Nikta.  
**What success looks like:**
- They gain a **reliable route-token / rite-key** that lets them continue west without getting lost.
- They meet (or are observed by) a **minor divinity** who sets a future price.
- They leave with urgency intact: “Nikta is reachable, but not for free.”

**What failure changes:**
- They lose time/resources and **Witherwild Bloom ticks +1**, *or*
- They take a “shortcut” that creates a **future complication** (owed debt, curse-mark, or pursuit).

## Prep in 60 seconds
- **Open with:** The land “sings” — wind through reeds that repeats the party’s words a beat later, slightly wrong.
- **Spotlight:** Find (Nikta faith/balance), and one of: Harlow’s paranoia scar / Flawsee’s curse anxiety.
- **Clock pressures to bring on-screen:**  
  - **Witherwild Bloom** (the land learns; navigation costs)  
  - **Fanewraith Balance** (something she was holding back leaks through as an omen)

## Scene 1 — Main Scene: The Rule of the Path (60–80 min)
**Purpose:** Make travel the challenge; reward patience/ritual over force.  
**Location:** A wide reed-flat / spring-meadow that should be easy to cross, but isn’t.  
**Environment (Traversal):** “Reedflat Labyrinth” (custom, light-weight)
Pick 3 features:
- **Echoing Steps (Passive):** If they backtrack without changing approach, they return to the same spot. (Signals “logic won’t save you alone.”)
- **Borrowed Direction (Passive):** A PC can make a Knowledge or Instinct roll to “read” the pattern—success reveals a rule; success w/ Fear reveals it but costs 1 Stress.
- **The Song Demands a Verse (Action):** If they argue/force, the reeds lash (minor damage + condition like Vulnerable/Restrained for a moment).
- **Hidden Hollows (Reaction):** On failure with Fear, someone steps into a sink of soft ground (lose gear OR mark Stress OR fall behind).

**Countdown:** **Progress (10)** — *“Earn the Westward Line”*  
- Tick **down** when they: honor a pattern, offer a small sacrifice, use a divinity-friendly approach, or accept a cost.  
- Tick **up** when they: try to brute-force, ignore warnings, or fight the environment.

**Rules they can discover (examples—use 2–3):**
- “You can only move forward after you tell the truth.”  
- “The path takes what you refuse to name.”  
- “Borrow direction from someone else (Help an Ally / spend Hope together).”  
- “A vow spoken aloud becomes a key.”

**End of Scene reward:**  
They earn a **Rite-Key: WESTWARD LINE** (a concrete token/phrase/sigil).  
Mechanically: once per session during travel toward Nikta, they can spend this key to **avoid one navigation consequence** (or to gain advantage on the next travel roll). This becomes your “permission slip” to keep momentum.

## Scene 2 — Optional Encounter: The Listener in the Reeds (0–45 min)
**Trigger:** Only if they linger, camp here, or “take the easy way.”  
**Encounter purpose:** Show the edge is alive, but keep combat short.

**Adversaries to preload (freshcutgrass):**
Choose ONE package:
- **Package A (short + scary):** *Dire Bat* (Tier 3 Skulk)  
- **Package B (softer):** 1 Tier 2 beast + 2–4 Tier 1 minions (if you want a lighter fight)

**Terrain hook:**  
- Movement is weird: anyone who moves **Far** risks becoming briefly isolated (narrative separation, not a whole minigame).

**Fast win condition:**  
- If PCs deal **Major damage** to the threat OR use the Rite-Key cleverly, it withdraws. (This keeps the encounter from becoming a 90-minute slug.)

**Clock outcome:**  
- If they kill it violently: Bloom +1 (the land remembers blood)  
- If they repel it with rite/bargain: Fanewraith Balance -1 (they unknowingly support containment)

## Scene 3 — Travel / Pressure Beat: A Minor Divinity’s Price (10–20 min)
**Purpose:** Introduce minor divinity presence without a full negotiation scene.  
A small divinity manifests indirectly (footprints of flowers, a lantern that wasn’t there, a voice in a puddle).

Offer a **single, clean bargain**:
- “I will keep your route from folding once… but you must carry a name I give you until you reach Nikta.”

If they accept:
- Give a simple boon: **advantage on the next travel/navigation roll** or **ignore one environmental consequence** next session.
- Record a debt: **DIVINITY DEBT (1)** (you can cash this later as a complication).

If they refuse:
- No boon, but no debt. Still let them progress; just make it rougher.

## Rewards / Reveals
- **Information revealed:** The Witherwild “obeys” patterns; vows and truth have mechanical weight here.
- **Material rewards:** Rite-Key: Westward Line
- **Relationship shifts:** Minor divinity attention begins; the party is “noticed.”
