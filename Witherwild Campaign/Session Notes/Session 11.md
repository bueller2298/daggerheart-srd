# SCENE 1 — NAVIGATION: “WHICH WEST IS WEST?” (20–35 min)

### Set the scene (read aloud)
*The woods ahead don’t feel like a border. They feel like a mouth. The grass behind you still whispers, but the trees in front of you don’t whisper at all. They stand too still—as if listening for the moment you step under them.*

*Your map has a west. The land has its own.*

## Visible Countdowns (player-facing)
### Objective (6): **STAY ON THE NIKTA LINE**
### Threat (4): **THE LAND MISDIRECTS**
You can show these as two tracks on the table. Don’t label “good/bad.” The names do the work.

## The navigation roll (do this now)
**Call for:** one **group navigation check** led by whoever is guiding.  
- Suggested approach: **Instinct** (reading wind/terrain), **Knowledge** (map/lore), or **Presence** (ritual/vow to align intent).  
- **Difficulty:** Moderate-to-Hard for Edge travel. (If you want a single number: set it as “Hard.”)
- **Help:** others can assist with short, specific contributions (map reading, flying recon, listening to hum).

### What success looks like (DM)
- Tick **STAY ON THE NIKTA LINE** by 2 (or reduce misdirect by 1).
- They choose between **two viable routes** (you still get agency/choice).

### What Fear/Failure looks like (DM)
- Tick **THE LAND MISDIRECTS** by 1–2.
- They still move forward, but the route becomes a **wrong turn with purpose**:
  - either **toward Hidden Hill’s outer approaches** (Harlow clock),
  - or **toward an Isle-of-Twelve omen site** (Flawsee clock),
  - or **toward a stitched seam landmark** (Fanewraith/Bloom pressure).

## Flight integration (for Restore)
If Restore takes to the air, he can roll as part of the check, but:
- On Hope: he spots a real landmark (ridge line / ruin / smoke / unusual canopy break).
- On Fear: he sees a **false landmark** (like Session 10) that feels “in-between” and costs time.

### Read aloud if flight reveals the “wrongness”
*From above, the forest doesn’t form a single canopy. It forms overlapping canopies—like the land can’t agree where the trees are supposed to be. West is visible… and still uncertain.*

# SCENE 2 — THE WRONG TURN (or THE RIGHT TURN) (45–70 min)
This scene is where you **weave backstories**. Choose Route A or B based on the navigation outcome.

## ROUTE A (If they stay on line or “misdirect west”): FLawsee’s Omen Site
**Purpose:** advance **Flawsee’s Curse** and show “knowledge has a price.”

### Read aloud
*The trees open onto a clearing that shouldn’t exist—an oval of ground where the grass refuses to grow. Twelve stones ring it, each one split by roots that have grown through the cracks like fingers.*

*In the center: jewelry. Not treasure—offerings. Broken clasps. Single earrings. Rings missing their stones.*

**What it implies (DM-only):**
- This is a “Twelve-adjacent” echo. It is not the Isle itself, but it’s a **pressure point**.
- The land is telling Flawsee: “your curse is part of the map.”

**Spotlight prompt (ask at table):**
- “Flawsee—what piece of jewelry do you still carry that you never wear?”
- “What do you fear learning about your family?”

**Advance personal clock:**
- If Flawsee interacts with the ring, stones, or makes an offering: **Flawsee’s Curse +1**
- If Flawsee refuses and walks away: no tick, but Bloom pressure increases (the land notices avoidance).

**Optional complication (non-combat):**
- A whispered bargain: trade a *true detail* about Flawsee’s family for a direction clue.
- If accepted: they get a strong navigation correction (tick objective), but curse +1.

---

## ROUTE B (If misdirected “north-ish” or toward people): Hidden Hill’s Outer Signs
**Purpose:** advance **Hidden Hill Boils** and put Harlow’s past on-screen.

### Read aloud
*You find the first sign before you see any wall: a hammered iron charm nailed to a tree, shaped like a small hill. Someone has scratched a warning into the bark around it—fresh enough that the sap still weeps.*

*“DO NOT BRING THE SICK HOME.”*

**What it implies (DM-only):**
- This is Young Chief paranoia made physical.
- You’re near a trade-path or outlying watch zone, not the settlement itself.

**Spotlight prompt:**
- “Harlow—what was the last *good* memory you had of Hidden Hill before it turned?”
- “What would it cost you to ask for help here?”

**Advance personal clock:**
- If the party chooses to approach / investigate / leave a message: **Hidden Hill Boils +1**
- If they turn away to avoid the risk: no tick, but Haven’s grip can tick (routes militarize when locals isolate).

**Optional encounter (social, not combat):**
- A Hidden Hill lookout (young, scared, armed) confronts them at range.  
  - Win condition: talk them down or prove identity without violence.  
  - Failure with Fear: the lookout fires a warning shot and disappears → later consequences.

# SCENE 3 — DIRN’S BIRD (20–35 min)
This is your “rumor update” replacement: intelligence delivered without deep settlements.

## Arrival (read aloud)
*The wind shifts. The leaves don’t. Something small cuts through the air anyway.*

*A bird drops from the gray sky—a raven-sized thing with too-still wings. Its eyes aren’t glass. They’re polished stone. It lands on a branch like a nail being hammered in.*

*It opens its beak, and a voice you recognize spills out—not the bird’s voice. Kreil Dirn’s.*

## What the bird is (DM-only)
A **bone-and-clockwork courier** keyed to find the party. It can carry:
- a short message
- a small token
- a directive or offer
- and a “thin threat” (you are being watched)

## The message (read aloud, as Dirn)
Pick 2–3 lines depending on tone:

*“You’re further west than my maps can promise you. That means you’re either useful… or already dead.”*  
*“Phylax has moved a new escort into the bog corridor. They’re not looking for flowers anymore. They’re looking for people.”*  
*“Do not trust ‘help’ offered by the small gods. Every stitch tightens something else.”*  
*“If you find a seam in the world—mark it. Do not bleed on it. Do you understand me?”*

### Include one actionable intel nugget (choose one)
- **Nikta direction correction:** “West-southwest. Follow moonlit sap-glow in the tree line.”  
- **Phylax clue:** “A ‘quiet grove’ where nothing rots has appeared near the frontier. That’s his work.”  
- **Haven pressure:** “A patrol is days behind you—guided by someone who knows Fanewick.”  
- **Hidden Hill:** “Your old home is tightening its gates. Fear is winning.”

## The bird’s “extra” (hooks)
Choose one:
- It drops a **map scrap** with a single circled landmark.
- It drops a **sealed charm** that can be used once to call the bird again (but costs a clock tick—Dirn gets something too).
- It repeats one phrase twice, slightly wrong the second time (field interference has reached even this).

# ENDING DECISION (close on a choice)
Give them three clear options and end the session on the pick.

### Present these options (DM prompt)
1) **Chase the correct line** toward Nikta immediately (fast, risky).  
2) **Investigate the wrong-turn site** deeper (backstory payoff, but time cost).  
3) **Follow Dirn’s intel** toward the “quiet grove” / seam marking (bigger plot, Phylax tie-in).

### Closing read aloud
*The bird’s stone eyes don’t blink. The woods ahead don’t breathe. The grass behind you still hums.*

*Somewhere west, Nikta waits.*

*And somewhere else, something is still stitching the world shut—pulling tighter every time you force your way through.*
