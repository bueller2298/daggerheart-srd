# SESSION 11 — THE BIRD, THE WRONG TURN, AND THE PATH THAT WON’T AGREE
**Act:** Act 2 (early)  
**Band:** Edge (with “Mid islands” that survived)  
**Length:** 2–2.5 hours  
**Focus:** Personal backstories forward + personal clocks tick. Navigation uncertainty. Dirn’s bird delivers offscreen intel.  
**Starting clocks (canon):** Bloom 5/8, Fanewraith Balance 4/8, Phylax 3/6, Haven Grip 2/6, Hidden Hill 3/6, Flawsee Curse 1/8

**Player-facing countdown standard:** Objective **6**, Threat **4** (visible), no “good/bad” labels.

---

## SESSION 10 → SESSION 11 TRANSITION (read aloud)
*The stones are behind you now, but the feeling of stitched air clings to your teeth. The Fields never truly stop humming—you just get far enough away that the sound becomes memory instead of weather.*

*Ahead, the grass finally breaks. Trees rise in a dense line, their trunks pale with too-fast growth. Somewhere beyond them is Nikta… and somewhere behind you, something is still stitching the world shut.*

*And you can’t shake the thought that killing the ooze made the land grow stronger.*

---

## QUICK RUN (DM)
**Three beats:**
1) **Navigation check up front** → determines which “wrong turn” you land on (both are useful).
2) **Backstory scene** keyed to that route (Harlow OR Flawsee, with Restore layered in).
3) **Dirn’s bird** arrives with actionable intel + pressure; end on a decision.

**Rail-lite structure:** in each beat, present 2–3 clear options. Players pick; you don’t wait for them to invent.

---

# SCENE 1 — NAVIGATION: “WHICH WEST IS WEST?” (20–35 min)

### READ ALOUD
*The woods ahead don’t feel like a border. They feel like a mouth. The grass behind you still whispers, but the trees in front of you don’t whisper at all. They stand too still—as if listening for the moment you step under them.*

*Your map has a west. The land has its own.*

## Player-facing countdowns (visible)
### Objective (6): **STAY ON THE NIKTA LINE**
### Threat (4): **THE LAND MISDIRECTS**

## The navigation check (do this now)
**Environment Tier:** 3 → **DC 15**  
**Call for:** ONE group navigation check led by whoever is guiding. Choose ONE approach:
- **Instinct (DC 15):** reading wind, slope, canopy breaks, animal sign
- **Knowledge (DC 15):** map reading, lore, stars/sunline, field-to-forest transitions
- **Presence (DC 15):** ritual intent, vow, “say it true / together” anchoring to keep the land honest

**Help actions (each helper describes 1 concrete thing):**
- Restore: brief flight recon (see Flight rules below)
- Flawsee: lore of the region / read the “rules” the land is using
- Harlow: survival logic / route discipline / keeping the party together

### Flight rules (if Restore scouts)
Restore can scout as part of the check.
- On **Hope:** he spots a real landmark (ridge notch, creek-cut, ruin stump, canopy scar) → tick Objective +1
- On **Fear:** he sees a false landmark / “in-between” west → tick Threat +1

### Outcomes (keep it clean and explicit)
- **Success with Hope:** tick **STAY ON THE NIKTA LINE +2** and choose Route A or Route B (player choice).
- **Success with Fear:** tick **STAY ON THE NIKTA LINE +1** and tick **THE LAND MISDIRECTS +1**; choose route (player choice), but it costs time.
- **Failure:** tick **THE LAND MISDIRECTS +2**; you still move forward, but you land in the “wrong turn” route first (you choose based on what you want to spotlight).

> DM note: never say “tick.” Describe it: “the hum steadies / the air tightens / the trees lean wrong.”

---

## Scene 1 signposts (give them options without spelling mechanics)
As they start moving, give 2–3 quick sensory prompts:
- A **sap-glow** seam in bark that looks like moonlight trapped in resin (suggests: “follow that”)
- A **stone marker** with an old pilgrim palm symbol (suggests: “offer / vow / respect”)
- A **direction that feels easy** but wrong (suggests: “shortcut with cost”)

# SCENE 2 — THE ROUTE YOU GOT (Backstory-heavy, 45–70 min)

Choose **Route A** or **Route B** based on the nav result (or pick the one you want most).

---

## ROUTE A — FLAWSEE’S OMEN RING (Curse pressure point)
**Purpose:** advance Flawsee’s personal clock and reinforce “knowledge has a price.”

### READ ALOUD
*The trees open onto a clearing that shouldn’t exist—an oval of ground where grass refuses to grow. Twelve stones ring it, each one split by roots that have grown through cracks like fingers.*

*In the center: jewelry. Not treasure—offerings. Broken clasps. Single earrings. Rings missing their stones. A neat little pile, like someone has been paying the land in ornaments instead of blood.*

### Present 3 obvious options (rail-lite)
1) **Investigate carefully** (learn something, risk curse tick)
2) **Make an offering** (safer, but you give up something)
3) **Leave it alone** (no immediate cost, but you lose guidance)

### Roll prompts (only if they choose to engage)
**Environment Tier:** 2 → **DC 13**
- **Knowledge (DC 13):** identify pattern / meaning (Hope = clarity, Fear = costly clarity)
- **Instinct (DC 13):** sense the “right” object to touch/avoid

**On success with Hope:** gain a clear omen (see below)  
**On success with Fear:** omen + Flawsee Curse +1  
**On failure:** Flawsee Curse +1 and the field “misdirect” pressure returns (Threat +1 next nav check)

### The omen payload (choose 1)
- “The curse is not a single spell. It’s a **contract**—someone *agreed*.”
- “The Twelve don’t want your death. They want your **continuation**—as a vessel.”
- “Your family didn’t vanish. They were **reassigned**.”

### Backstory spotlight prompts (ask at table)
- “Flawsee, what piece of jewelry do you carry that you never wear?”
- “What do you fear you’ll learn about who cursed your family?”
- “What would you trade for the name of the curser?”

### Clock movement (make it explicit in your notes)
- If Flawsee interacts meaningfully: **Flawsee Curse +1**
- If he makes an offering instead: **Flawsee Curse stays**, but **Witherwild Bloom does NOT spike** (land respected)

---

## ROUTE B — HIDDEN HILL’S OUTER WARNING (Harlow pressure point)
**Purpose:** advance Hidden Hill and put Harlow’s past in the scene.

### READ ALOUD
*You find the first sign before you see any wall: a hammered iron charm nailed to a tree, shaped like a small hill. Someone has scratched a warning into the bark around it—fresh enough that sap still weeps.*

*“DO NOT BRING THE SICK HOME.”*

### Present 3 obvious options (rail-lite)
1) **Approach openly** (risk confrontation, potential ally)
2) **Scout quietly** (safer, but might be misread)
3) **Leave a sign/message and move on** (progress without a scene)

### Social encounter: a lookout (optional, but good for new players)
A young Hidden Hill lookout appears at range, arrow nocked, voice shaking.

**Environment Tier:** 2 → **DC 13** (Presence) to calm them / prove identity / avoid violence  
- Success (Hope): they share one useful fact + don’t report you
- Success (Fear): they share a fact but report “outsiders” later → **Hidden Hill Boils +1**
- Failure: warning shot + retreat → **Hidden Hill Boils +1** and Haven Grip might rise later (“outsiders = plague” story spreads)

### Backstory prompts (ask at table)
- “Harlow—what was the last good day in Hidden Hill before paranoia won?”
- “What name would the lookout recognize: Clyde, Harlow, or something older?”
- “Do you want your family found… or left untouched?”

### Clock movement
- If they engage this route meaningfully: **Hidden Hill Boils +1** (the region heats up because they’re back on the map)

# SCENE 3 — DIRN’S BIRD (20–35 min)

**Purpose:** deliver offscreen consequences and a concrete next-step without needing deep-wild NPC settlements.

### READ ALOUD (arrival)
*The wind shifts. The leaves don’t. Something small cuts through the air anyway.*

*A bird drops from the gray sky—a raven-sized thing with too-still wings. Its eyes aren’t glass. They’re polished stone. It lands on a branch like a nail being hammered in.*

*It opens its beak, and a voice you recognize spills out—not the bird’s voice. Kreil Dirn’s.*

## Quick ID roll (optional, if they test it)
**Tier:** 2 → **DC 13** (Knowledge or Instinct)  
- Hope: “clockwork/bone courier, keyed to you”  
- Fear: “you feel watched; message glitches once”

## Dirn’s message (read 2–3 lines)
Pick based on what you want to emphasize:
- *“You’re further west than my maps can promise. That means you’re either useful… or already dead.”*
- *“Phylax has moved a new escort into the bog corridor. They’re not hunting flowers anymore. They’re hunting people.”*
- *“The land is feeding. Don’t let it learn you.”*
- *“If you find a seam—mark it. Don’t bleed on it.”*

## Actionable intel nugget (choose 1)
- **Nikta line:** *“West-southwest. Follow sap-glow in the tree line. If the glow stops, you’re off-course.”*
- **Phylax clue:** *“A ‘quiet grove’ has appeared—no insects, no rot, no birds. That’s engineered.”*
- **Haven pressure:** *“A patrol is days behind you—guided by someone who knows Fanewick.”*

## The bird drops a token (choose 1)
- A **map scrap** with one landmark circled (you decide which route it supports)
- A **one-use charm** to call the bird again later (cost: Dirn gets a favor; you choose the favor later)
- A **black thread** that tightens if they lie (Dirn’s way of forcing truth later)

---

# ENDING DECISION (close session on a choice)
Present three clear options and end on their pick:
1) **Push the Nikta line now** (fast, risky)
2) **Follow the personal hook** deeper (Hidden Hill or Twelve omen)
3) **Follow Dirn’s intel** toward the “quiet grove” / seam marking

### Closing read aloud
*The bird’s stone eyes don’t blink. The woods ahead don’t breathe. The grass behind you still hums.*

*Somewhere west, Nikta waits.*

*And somewhere else, something is still stitching the world shut—pulling tighter every time you force your way through.*
