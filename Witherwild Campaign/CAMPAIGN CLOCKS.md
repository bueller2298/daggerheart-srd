# CAMPAIGN CLOCKS — WITHERWILD

Use these clocks to keep pressure on a medium-fast cadence (you meet every ~3 weeks). Advance clocks based on **player choices**, not time alone, but each session should *usually* advance at least one clock by 1 tick.

> **Tick guidance:** most clocks are **6 or 8 ticks**. “Advance” means tick down toward completion; “Escalate” means tick up toward disaster (pick one direction per clock and stick with it).

---

## 1) THE FANWRAITH’S WESTERN WORK (8-tick countdown)
**What it represents:** The Fanewraith’s distributed forces (and her own movement) closing on Nikta and preparing the Sowing Eye play.

- **0/8 → 8/8:** The plan is ready and executed (or its substitute).
- **Advance when:** the party rests too long in the wild, loses the trail, chooses detours, or burns bridges with potential guides.
- **Hold/slow when:** the party destroys an agent/simulacrum, seizes a key ritual component, wins an ally in her coalition, or forces the Fanewraith to respond elsewhere.

**At 4/8:** The party starts seeing “petal signs” ahead of them; agents have already passed through.
**At 6/8:** A ritual site is prepared; the party can feel the land bracing.
**At 8/8:** Sowing Eye event occurs (full, partial, or substituted—Nikta survives per GM line).

---

## 2) HAVEN’S IRON FOOTPRINT (6-tick escalation)
**What it represents:** Haven militarizes routes, hardens checkpoints, and expands extraction/security into Fanewick.

- **0/6 → 6/6:** Haven establishes a forward presence that reshapes travel (and becomes a secondary antagonist or forced ally).
- **Advance when:** the party kills Haven personnel publicly, leaves evidence, sides against Haven interests, or Haven learns the party’s route.
- **Hold/slow when:** the party maintains deniable actions, brokers temporary truces, feeds misinformation, or gains leverage over Dirn/Phylax.

**At 2/6:** Patrols increase; travel papers and interrogations become common.
**At 4/6:** A “special unit” enters Fanewick with a targeted mission.
**At 6/6:** A major route/bridge/town is occupied or locked down; supplies become a weapon.

---

## 3) PHY LAX’S REAPING EYE PROJECT (8-tick countdown)
**What it represents:** Archmage Phylax actively uses the Reaping Eye (cure attempt, weaponization, ritual, or political gambit).

- **0/8 → 8/8:** The project goes live (success or catastrophic side-effect).
- **Advance when:** the party ignores Haven entirely, fails to gather intel, or Haven remains unchallenged.
- **Hold/slow when:** the party steals components, exposes the project, recruits an insider, or forces Phylax into defensive secrecy.

**At 3/8:** Rumors of “miracles” or “failed tests” leak.
**At 5/8:** A field consequence appears in Fanewick (new Withered behavior, strange weather, sickly spring intensification).
**At 8/8:** The Reaping Eye becomes an active endgame lever.

---

## 4) THE WITHERWILD’S APPETITE (6-tick escalation)
**What it represents:** The land’s ability to process bodies, memories, and meaning grows stronger (Fields are the proof-text).

- **0/6 → 6/6:** The Witherwild becomes a dominant antagonist force in traversal and combat.
- **Advance when:** the party burns the land, uses crude magic, makes violent choices that spill blood in sacred places, or fails critical navigation.
- **Hold/slow when:** the party makes restorative choices, bargains respectfully with divinities, completes cleansing/ritual work, or returns stolen balance to a place.

**At 2/6:** “Soft hazards” (song, pollen, time drift) become frequent.
**At 4/6:** The land starts creating “answers” (cocoons, thorn walls, living traps).
**At 6/6:** A major region becomes a living set-piece dungeon (terrain has turns).

---

## 5) FLAWSEE’S FAMILY CURSE (6-tick countdown)
**What it represents:** The curse that claimed Flawsee’s family progresses toward a point of no return and pulls the campaign toward the Isle of the Twelve.

- **0/6 → 6/6:** The curse reaches a crisis and forces a hard choice (detour, bargain, sacrifice, or irreversible change).
- **Advance when:** the party delays resolution, uses risky magic on Flawsee, or engages in Witherwild-tainted bargains.
- **Hold/slow when:** the party secures a lead/relic, reaches an archivist/divinity for insight, or takes a direct step toward the Isle.

**At 3/6:** A visible symptom worsens; the curse interferes with a crucial scene.
**At 5/6:** A “family echo” appears (memory/vision/entity) offering a dangerous shortcut.
**At 6/6:** Isle-of-the-Twelve detour becomes unavoidable if they want to save what can be saved.

---

# How to Advance Clocks (rules of thumb)
- **Each session:** advance 1–2 clocks by **+1** based on choices and consequences.
- **Big choices:** should advance a clock by **+2** (or advance two clocks by +1).
- **Meaningful wins:** can “hold” a clock for a session, or reduce a clock by 1 if the fiction strongly supports it.

# Off-Ramp Clock Effects (Haven vs Opposition)
- If the party **sides with Haven**, reduce difficulty of travel logistics but accelerate:
  - **Haven’s Iron Footprint** (+1 per session automatically)
  - **Fanewraith’s Western Work** (she responds faster to state pressure)
- If the party **opposes Haven**, accelerate:
  - **Haven’s Iron Footprint** (crackdowns)
  - **Witherwild’s Appetite** (more deep-wild travel)
  - But you can slow **Phylax’s Project** if they actively sabotage/steal.
