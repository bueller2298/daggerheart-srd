# ACT 1 — THE HUNT AND THE MISREAD ENEMY (Completed / Closing)

## Act 1 thesis
The party is hired/propelled to stop a “monster” (Fanewraith)… and learns she is **not a normal antagonist**—and that stopping her is not the same as saving Fanewick.

## Tentpole beats (what already happened / should be canon)
1) **The Contract of Necessity**
   - Kreil Dirn frames the Fanewraith as an existential threat and turns the party loose as his deniable solution.
2) **The Trail in Alula**
   - The hunt becomes personal: witnesses, rumors, and recruitment patterns suggest the Fanewraith is *organizing* people, not merely killing them.
3) **First Proof of the Supernatural “Composite”**
   - The hut encounter: the simulacrum cycles faces/identities and dissolves into petals—establishing *she is plural* and *returns to source* when “killed.”

## Act 1 revelation (the handoff into Act 2)
- **The party can’t stay in “hunt mode.”** They now have to navigate an ecosystem that is itself an actor, and a “villain” whose motives may be protective.

## Escalating threats introduced in Act 1
- Haven’s occupation/pressure (routes, patrols, extraction)
- The Witherwild as **environmental weapon**
- The Fanewraith as **distributed presence** (simulacra, agents, and cult-ish devotion)

# ACT 2 — THE WESTERN MARCH AND THE TRUE SHAPE OF BALANCE (Main body of campaign)

## Act 2 thesis
The party races toward Nikta to stop the Fanewraith—while learning the Fanewraith is a **liminal safeguard** holding back something worse, and Haven’s “solution” is not neutral.

## Act 2 tentpoles (set-piece beats)
### 2.1 The Fields That Eat
- The party crosses multiple “living systems” (Fields, bog gardens, thorn corridors).
- **Revelation drip:** the Witherwild isn’t only growth—it is **selection, digestion, and memory**.

### 2.2 Minor Divinities, Major Costs
- Introduce 2–4 **minor divinities** (helpful, petty, or dangerous).
- Each offers **navigation/boons** in exchange for a **price** (Hope, a memory, a promise, a name, an item, a bond).
- **Revelation drip:** some divinities speak of the Fanewraith with fear and reverence—like a coalition that *keeps the worst sealed*.

### 2.3 The Fanewraith’s Origin: The Choir and the Woman
- Confirm (through divinities, relics, or witnesses) that the Fanewraith is a **composite entity**:
  - multiple minor divinities + a human woman bound into a single “balancer”
- **Reframing beat:** killing/stopping her outright removes a stabilizing pressure.

### 2.4 Haven’s Counter-Move
- Kreil Dirn’s network tightens its grip: “proof,” demands, or a second mission arrives mid-journey.
- **Archmage Phylax**’s agenda becomes legible:
  - the Reaping Eye is being used for something *beyond* storage (a cure attempt, a weaponization attempt, a god-tech ritual, or a political gambit).
- This is where the party is forced to decide whether Haven is “ally,” “tool,” or “secondary antagonist.”

### 2.5 Nikta’s Domain: The Shepherd Without Her Eye
- The party reaches a threshold region that behaves like a **court** (not a lair).
- Nikta is alive, powerful, and constrained—less “quest NPC,” more “cosmic stakeholder.”
- **Major revelation:** restoring balance is not “put the eye back” simple; the missing cycle has already bred a new equilibrium that will fight to remain.

## Act 2 climax options (choose based on player choices)
A) **They beat the Fanewraith to Nikta**
- They prevent the plucking of the Sowing Eye… but trigger another consequence (Witherwild spike, divinity backlash, Haven escalation).
B) **They arrive too late (your preferred hard line preserved: Nikta lives)**
- Nikta survives, but the Fanewraith has *done something irreversible*:
  - the Sowing Eye is damaged/partially removed/“sung” into a ritual; or
  - a substitute is installed; or
  - a bond is forced that changes Nikta’s role.
C) **They ally with the Fanewraith**
- They help implement a version of her plan, but insist on guardrails, alternatives, or a second step.

# ACT 3 — BITTERSWEET RESTORATION AND THE COST OF SAVING (Endgame)

## Act 3 thesis
No ending is clean: the party must choose which “good” to sacrifice—Haven’s survival, Fanewick’s autonomy, Nikta’s wholeness, or the party’s own humanity.

## Act 3 tentpoles
### 3.1 The Bigger Threat Emerges
- The truth becomes unavoidable: the Fanewraith was **holding back** a deeper catastrophe.
- Options for the “real threat” (pick what fits your tone):
  1) A **Withered gestalt** that forms when the land’s “processing” is disrupted.
  2) A **Fallen-cult style** corruption exploiting the Eyes’ imbalance.
  3) A **Haven divinity** (Shun’Aush / stone-tomb dynamics) making the problem worse by “sealing” too hard.

### 3.2 The Reaping Eye Gambit
- Phylax’s project comes online (or fails catastrophically).
- The Reaping Eye becomes a **lever** the party can seize, sabotage, or bargain over.

### 3.3 The Balance Choice (Finale)
- The party executes a final plan that answers:
  - What replaces the missing cycle?
  - Who holds the authority to decide?
  - What must be surrendered so the land can breathe again?

## Finale image (bittersweet)
- A restored seasonal *motion* returns… but someone/something pays:
  - A divinity fades, a town is abandoned, a PC loses something precious, or Haven’s foothold collapses into a harsher kind of order.

# Off-Ramps and Branches (Haven vs Opposition)

## If the party sides with Haven (openly)
**Tone shift:** militarized “expedition” campaign.
- **Act 2 changes:** more route control, more structured missions, more human antagonists.
- **Act 3 endgame:** restoring balance becomes a “state project” (which risks repeating the original sin).

Key consequences:
- Haven accelerates troop movement and builds a forward operating presence.
- The Fanewraith becomes a direct enemy (and her death risks unleashing the deeper threat faster).
- Nikta’s court treats the party as *agents of the invasion* unless they earn a costly pardon.

## If the party opposes Haven (openly)
**Tone shift:** insurgent/folk-horror road campaign.
- **Act 2 changes:** more covert travel, more divinity bargains, more sabotage.
- **Act 3 endgame:** balance restoration is achieved *despite* Haven, not through it.

Key consequences:
- Dirn’s relationship becomes hostile (or desperate blackmail).
- Haven routes become dangerous checkpoints (pressure cooker scenes).
- The party gains access to deeper wild allies (minor divinities, hidden communities, Fanewraith sympathizers).
